Sorghum

sorghum rust -Puccinia purpurea

Sorghum rust is a fungal disease caused by the pathogen Puccinia purpurea that affects sorghum plants at all stages of growth. It's found in all areas of the world where sorghum is grown.

Symptoms

Initial symptoms appear as small purple-red or tan spots on both upper and lower surfaces of the lower leaves. These spots enlarge to form raised pustules that rupture to release masses of reddish-brown spores. The pustules spread to all leaves and may also be found on the leaf sheaths ,stem.

Favourable Condition

  • High humidity: Sorghum rust thrives in high relative humidity, ideally close to 100%
  • Cool temperatures: Sorghum rust prefers cool temperatures, around 10–12°C
  • Rain and dew: Sorghum rust is favored by rain and dew.
  • Cloudy weather: Cool night temperatures and cloudy weather can make sorghum rust worse.

Management
  • The application of fungicides can be beneficial when used on susceptible varieties. Products based on hexaconazole (0.1%), difenconazole (0.1 %) and propiconazole (0.1 %) can be used to control the disease. Two sprays of these fungicides at 15 days interval immediately after appearance of symptoms is recommended.
  • use resistant varieties like RSV2383,RSV2390,RSV2393,RSV2394,RSV2395.
  • Spray Mancozeb at 1kg/ha. Repeat fungicidal application after 10 days
